# The Knowledge–Practice Gap in Islamic Ritual Observance

## A Cross-Sectional Study of Wudu Knowledge, Visible Practice, and Mosque-Level Teaching Exposure in Riyadh

**Status:** Draft Proposal (Pre-Pilot)

**Research Stage:** Phase 1 – Research Foundation & Instrument Development

**Location:** Riyadh, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia

**Author:** Omar Emad

---

## Overview

This repository documents an ongoing academic research project investigating the relationship between:

- Procedural knowledge of wudu (ablution)
- Visible indicators of religious practice
- Mosque-level religious teaching exposure
- Demographic and educational factors

The study adopts a **Knowledge–Attitude–Practice (KAP)**-inspired framework adapted to the context of Islamic ritual observance.

The current phase focuses on developing the academic foundation, literature review, fiqh evidence base, research methodology, and survey instruments before pilot testing.

---

## Research Objectives

The project aims to answer the following questions:

1. What is the level of correct knowledge of obligatory and sunnah acts of wudu among mosque attendees in Riyadh?
2. Is there a relationship between visible religious practice and procedural knowledge?
3. Does imam-led teaching at the mosque level influence individual knowledge?
4. Do knowledge scores vary across demographic and language groups?

## Research Methodology

- Study Design: Cross-sectional survey
- Framework: Knowledge–Practice (KAP-inspired)
- Location: Riyadh, Saudi Arabia
- Population: Adult Muslim men attending congregational prayers
- Planned Sample Size: Approximately 600 participants from around 30 mosques
